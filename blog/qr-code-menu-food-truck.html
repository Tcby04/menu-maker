How to Create a QR Code Menu for Your Food Truck (Free, 5 Minutes)

Every food truck needs a menu customers can read fast. Paper menus get dirty, wear out, and cost money to reprint when prices change. A QR code menu solves all of that.

Here's how to create one for free using MenuMaker.

Create Your Free Menu →

Step 1: Go to MenuMaker

Visit menumaker.app — no account or signup needed. You can start building your menu immediately.

Step 2: Enter Your Truck Info

Add your food truck name and tagline. Pick a theme that matches your vibe — there are 6 color options including dark mode, sunset, and ocean.

Step 3: Add Menu Items

Create categories (like "Tacos", "Drinks", "Sides") and add items with names, prices, and optional descriptions. The free version supports up to 10 items.

Step 4: Generate Your QR Code

Click "Generate Menu" and you get:

  • A shareable link (works on any phone)
  • A QR code you can print and stick on your truck
  • A print-friendly menu page

Step 5: Print and Display

Print the QR code, laminate it, and display it where customers can see it. That's it — you're done.

Why Food Truck Owners Love QR Menus

  • Change prices instantly — no reprinting needed
  • Add/remove items — sold out? Hide it in seconds
  • Looks professional — 6 themes to match your brand
  • Works on any phone — iPhone, Android, anything
  • Fast — customers scan and see your menu in under 2 seconds

Do customers need to download an app?

No. Customers scan the QR code with their phone's camera and the menu opens in their browser instantly.

How much does it cost?

MenuMaker is free for basic use (1 menu, 10 items). Pro is $3/month for unlimited items, photos, custom backgrounds, and no branding.

Can I update my menu after printing the QR code?

Yes! The QR code stays the same forever. Just edit your menu in MenuMaker and customers always see the latest version. Change prices, add items, switch themes — the QR code doesn't change.

What if I have multiple trucks?

With Pro ($3/month), you get unlimited menus. Create one for each truck, each with its own QR code.
