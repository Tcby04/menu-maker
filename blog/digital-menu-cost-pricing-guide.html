Published March 2026 · 9 min read

Thinking about switching to a digital menu for your restaurant or food truck? The first question most owners ask is simple: how much is this going to cost me?

The range is wider than you'd expect — from completely free to $50+/month, depending on what features you need and which platform you choose. This guide breaks it all down so you don't overpay (or underserve your customers).

What Paid Plans Actually Get You

When you're ready to upgrade, here's what the typical $3-30/month range unlocks:

  • Unlimited menu items — The #1 reason to upgrade. If your menu has more than 10-15 items, you'll need this.
  • Photos on menu items — Dishes with photos sell 30% more on average. This feature alone can pay for the subscription.
  • Custom backgrounds — Match your brand instead of using a generic template.
  • Remove platform branding — Get rid of the "Made with [Platform]" footer for a more professional look.
  • Multiple menus — Separate lunch/dinner menus, drinks menu, specials board.

MenuMaker Pro costs just $3/month for all of the above — unlimited items, photos, custom backgrounds, and branding removal. That's less than the cost of printing a single paper menu.

Business Tier ($15 - $30/month)

This tier makes sense if you have multiple locations or a team that needs access:

  • Multiple locations under one account
  • Team member access (let your manager update the menu)
  • Analytics — see which items customers view most
  • Custom domain (menu.yourrestaurant.com)
  • Priority support

Enterprise Tier ($50+/month)

For restaurant chains and large operations:

  • API access for custom integrations
  • POS system integration
  • White-label options
  • Dedicated account manager
  • Multi-language menus

Platforms like Toast, TouchBistro, and Square for Restaurants offer these features, but they come bundled with POS systems and other restaurant management tools — you're paying for a lot more than just a menu.

Hidden Costs to Watch Out For

Not all pricing is as straightforward as it looks. Here are the gotchas:

⚠️ Transaction fees: Some platforms charge a percentage if customers order through the menu (2-5% per order). If you're just displaying a menu with no ordering, this doesn't apply — but read the fine print.

  • Per-location pricing: A platform that charges $15/month might charge $15/month per location. Three locations = $45/month, not $15.
  • Annual lock-in discounts: "Only $5/month!" often means $60 billed annually. If you cancel after 2 months, you may not get a refund.
  • Setup fees: Some enterprise platforms charge $100-500 just to get started.
  • Overage charges: Go over your item limit? Some platforms auto-upgrade you to a higher tier without asking.
  • Photo storage limits: "Unlimited items" but only 5 photos on the free plan. Adding photos to all your items requires upgrading.

What Features Are Actually Worth Paying For?

Not every feature deserves your money. Here's my honest ranking:

Worth It 👍

  • Unlimited items — Essential if your menu has more than 15 items. Non-negotiable for most restaurants.
  • Photos — Data consistently shows photos increase order value. If you sell food, show the food.
  • Easy updates — The whole point of a digital menu is fast changes. If the platform makes updates painful, it defeats the purpose.

Nice to Have 🤷

  • Custom backgrounds — Looks more professional, but most customers don't notice or care.
  • Analytics — Interesting data, but you can make good menu decisions without it.
  • Multiple menus — Useful for separate lunch/dinner, but you can work around it with one menu.

Probably Not Worth It ❌

  • Custom domain — Nice for branding, but a free subdomain works fine for most small restaurants.
  • Online ordering integration — Unless you're doing serious delivery volume, use DoorDash/UberEats directly instead.
  • POS integration — Overkill for food trucks and small cafés. Enter sales manually until you actually need it.
